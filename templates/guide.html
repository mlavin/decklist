Card and Deck Guide

Overview

Decks for the Pokémon Trading Card Game are made of 60 cards in total. These cards are a mixture of different types of cards including Pokémon, Trainer (Supporter, Item, Tool, Stadium), and Energy cards. Roughly these break down into 12-20 Pokémon, 25-35 Trainers, and 8-15 Energy. These ranges vary depending on the overall strategy of the deck and some decks may require cards outside of these general ranges. It should be noted that other than basic energy, you cannot have more than four of the same card with the same name in a deck.

Formats

Competitive Pokémon play is broken into two formats which dictate the set of cards which are allowable for play. These formats are Standard which includes the cards from Primal Clash (released Feb 2015) through the Sun and Moon base set (released Feb 2017) and Expanded which includes the cards from the Black and White base set (released April 2011) through the Sun and Moon base set (released Feb 2017). These formats rotate each September, dropping 3-5 of the oldest sets. Cards from older sets which have since been reprinted in legal set are still legal for play in that format as long as the wording on the card has not changed.

For example, the popular supporter N has been reprinted a number of times. While the original sets Noble Victories (released Nov 2011) and Dark Explorers (released May 2012) are no longer legal in Standard play, you can play those prints of N because they have the same wording at N when it was reprinted in Fates Collide (released May 2016) which is still legal in Standard.

For casual play, you may not care about whether cards are legal for tournament play, but it's worth keeping in mind that these cards are printed together and rotated to balance the game. It's also worth knowing that there might be other prints of the same card which could be cheaper or easier to find. This guide is written using examples from the Standard format but the principles apply to all formats. With larger formats such as Expanded there are more cards to choose from and more options for filling the roles needed to build a balanced and consitent deck.

Deck Strategy and Consistency

Decks are built around a particular strategy (or strategies) which will allow the player to win the game whether that is by taking their six prizes first or by discarding all of their oppenent's cards so that they cannot continue the game. Some decks do this through big, aggressive attacks which knock out most other Pokémon in a single hit. Other decks plan to consistently hit for two-hit knockouts. Some decks try to stop opponent's attacks from dealing much sometimes even any damage. Others try to deny their opponent any energy so they simply cannot attack. Whatever it is, a deck should have a clear strategy and the cards included in the deck should work to compliment that goal. Any card game involves a bit of randomness so decks are called consstent when they can effectively work towards their strategy regardless of the randomness of the draw.

Cards Roles

Attackers

Attacking with your Pokémon to knock out your opponent's Pokémon to take your six prizes is the most common way to win the game so it shouldn't be surprising that most decks are built around a single attacking Pokémon or a few complimentary Pokémon. Once you've identified a primary and secondary attacker in your deck, you can build the rest of your deck around supporting getting those Pokémon ready to attack. The types of cards you'll need with depend on the amount and type of energy required, the evolution stage of your attackers (basic, stage 1, stage 2, BREAK, Mega).
