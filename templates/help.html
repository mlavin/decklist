Getting Started Guide

What is This Site?

Primarily this site is a tool to help you price and purchase deck lists for the Pokémon Trading Card Game. If you don't have a list of your own then this guide can help you put one together or find one online.

Where Can I Get a Deck List?

Our deck price calculator supports lists generated in the export format from Pokémon TCG Online (PTCGO). You do not need to own the cards online to add them to a list and export them. Since it's an easy way to generate a list, many players will use this same format when sharing a list on their website, YouTube channel, or forum. Some places we recommend checking out are the OmniPoke YouTube channel, the Pokebeach Deck Garage, and the decklists on The Charizard Lounge.

If you are looking for a deck for competitive play, you can find the lists used by the top 8 Junior, Senior, and Master players at the Regional and Championship tournaments published by Play Pokémon. Following the link, you choose a recent event, select a division, and then a player from the dropdown menu. Each list has a "Copy Deck List" button which uses the PTCGO format.

How Can I Try a Deck Before Buying It?

Pokémon TCG Online (PTCGO) is an easy and free/cheap way to try out a deck before buying it. To get the cards you need you'll have to earn coins for packs or win tournaments for tradeable packs to trade for the cards you want. To accelerate this process you can also purchase codes to unlock tradeable booster packs online. These are significantly less expensive than the real life booster packs.

For testing in real life, you can print proxy versions of the cards. While these proxies won't be legal for tournament play, they can be perfect for your play testing. For generating proxies, we recommend Proxycroak which supports the same PTCGO list format.

Site Issues

Why Didn't My List Work?

The pricing calculator expects the cards to be listed roughly in the format provided by Pokémon TCG Online (PTCGO). The PTCGO format has each card on its own line and has the format:

{quantity} {card name} {set code} {card number}

such as "2 Shaymin-EX ROS 77". Since cards from the same name might appear in different sets it's important that the set code is included. Likewise the card number makes it clear exactly which card it is from the set. There have been sets which have multiple cards with the same name such as Mewtwo-EX from Breakthrough (61 and 62)

Why Is There No Price for Some Cards?

Most of the prices for cards used in competitive play should be available. The prices come from Amazon.com and we've tried our best to match up the cards with their listings and we work constantly to keep them up to date. However, there might be cards which could not be matched to any item available for sale. If you'd like to report a card which wasn't match please drop us an info@professordogwood.com.
